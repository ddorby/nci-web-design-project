Diarmuid

Introducing Diarmuid, the caffeine-fueled, froth-obsessed barista extraordinaire!

Born and raised in the rolling hills of Ireland, Diarmuid had a passion for all things coffee from a young age. He would spend hours tinkering with his parents' old espresso machine, perfecting his latte art and experimenting with new flavor combinations.

When he turned 18, Diarmuid packed up his bags (and his trusty milk frother) and headed off to college to pursue his dream of becoming a full-time barista. It wasn't long before he became the talk of the town, with customers flocking to his café just to catch a glimpse of his signature "Celtic knot" latte art.

But Diarmuid's love of coffee wasn't just confined to the café - he also spent his spare time roasting his own beans and concocting bizarre new coffee flavors in his basement laboratory. (Some of his more questionable creations include "Maple Bacon Mocha" and "Mushroom Latte.")

Despite his eccentricities, Diarmuid quickly became a beloved fixture on campus, known for his infectious enthusiasm and boundless energy. He could always be counted on to brighten up even the dreariest of Monday mornings with his jokes and banter - not to mention a perfectly brewed cup of joe.

So next time you're in need of a pick-me-up, be sure to swing by the café and say hi to Diarmuid. Who knows, he might even whip up a special concoction just for you (just don't ask for the mushroom latte).



Laura

Meet Laura.

Our resident coffee wizard and purveyor of caffeine-fueled joy! With her vibrant purple hair and endless collection of vintage band t-shirts, Laura is impossible to miss (or forget). But don't let her eccentric style fool you - when it comes to coffee, she's all business.

Hailing from the wild hills of north Italy, Laura's passion for coffee started early. She cut her teeth at a local coffee shop, honing her skills as a barista while experimenting with off-the-wall flavor combinations (her "Purple Rain" latte made with lavender and blackberry syrup is a customer favorite).

But Laura's coffee expertise doesn't stop at the espresso machine. In her free time, she's a self-taught coffee roaster, meticulously sourcing the highest quality beans from around the world and meticulously roasting them to perfection in her tiny apartment kitchen. Her latest creation, a blend of Ethiopian and Guatemalan beans dubbed "The Funky Chicken," is causing a stir among coffee connoisseurs.

When she's not brewing up a storm, Laura can be found jamming out to Prince or concocting new cocktails with her friends (her "coffee-infused Bloody Mary" is not for the faint of heart). She's also an avid collector of kitschy vintage coffee mugs, which she displays proudly on a bookshelf behind the bar.

So the next time you need a jolt of energy or a dose of laughter, swing by the café and say hi to Laura. She'll whip you up a cup of joe that's guaranteed to make your taste buds sing - and maybe even tell you a joke or two.
